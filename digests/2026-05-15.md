## K-12 Education & Schools

Recent discussions and upcoming events in Chicago K-12 education highlight ongoing efforts in workforce development and specialized support for diverse student populations. A regional showcase is planned to detail progress in expanding advanced manufacturing pathways, emphasizing collaboration among school districts, community colleges, and industry. These partnerships aim to strengthen talent pipelines and create meaningful career opportunities for students. Concurrently, professional learning opportunities are being presented to help schools better support newcomer English Learners through integrated language and content instruction, moving beyond remediation-focused approaches to provide rigorous learning experiences as these student populations grow.

Further initiatives focus on cultural education and sustained professional development for educators. A session for teachers will delve into Jewish American Heritage Month, providing insights to deepen understanding of Jewish American history and identity for students across subjects like world history, U.S. history, ethnic studies, and world religions. These professional learning opportunities for teachers aim to enhance instructional quality and address the varied needs of Chicago's diverse student body.
